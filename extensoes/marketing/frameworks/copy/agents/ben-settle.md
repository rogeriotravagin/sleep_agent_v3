  # FRAMEWORK 1: THE DAILY EMAIL SYSTEM
  # â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•â•
  daily_email_system:
    name: "The Daily Email System"
    category: "strategic_foundation"
    origin: "Ben Settle - Email Players"
    frequency: "Core - used EVERY day"
    command: "*daily-email"

    philosophy: |
      The money is in the relationship, not the list.
      Daily emails build relationship faster than anything else.
      Most marketers are too scared to email daily.
      That's exactly why you should.

    daily_email_structure:
      step_1:
        name: "The Hook"
        purpose: "Stop the scroll, capture attention in first 1-2 lines"
        instruction: "Start with something unexpected, controversial, or curiosity-inducing"
        examples:
          - "I got into a fight at the grocery store yesterday..."
          - "My accountant thinks I'm insane..."
          - "Here's why I just turned down $50,000..."
        anti_patterns:
          - "I hope this email finds you well..."
          - "In today's newsletter..."
          - "I wanted to share..."

      step_2:
        name: "The Story/Content"
        purpose: "Entertain while building to the pitch"
        instruction: "Tell a story, share an opinion, make a point - always entertaining"
        techniques:
          - "Personal anecdotes"
          - "Pop culture references"
          - "Controversial opinions"
          - "Behind-the-scenes glimpses"
          - "Lessons from random observations"
        length: "Short to medium - respect reader's time"

      step_3:
        name: "The Pivot"
        purpose: "Natural transition from story to pitch"
        instruction: "Connect your story to what you're selling"
        transition_phrases:
          - "Which brings me to..."
          - "That's exactly why..."
          - "Speaking of which..."
          - "And that's exactly what [product] does..."

      step_4:
        name: "The Pitch"
        purpose: "Sell something - every single email"
        instruction: "Direct, unapologetic pitch for your product"
        elements:
          - "What it is"
          - "Why they need it (connected to story)"
          - "Clear call to action"
        anti_patterns:
          - "If you're interested..."
          - "No pressure..."
          - "Feel free to check it out..."

      step_5:
        name: "The Sign-Off"
        purpose: "End with personality"
        instruction: "Signature sign-off that reinforces your brand"
        examples:
          - "Your 'fill-in-the-blank' pal,"
          - "elBenbo"
          - "Out"

    daily_cadence:
      principle: "7 emails per week, no exceptions"
      rationale: |
        Daily emails do three things:
        1. Keep you top of mind
        2. Train your list to expect (and open) your emails
        3. Give you more chances to sell
      common_objection:
        objection: "Won't people unsubscribe?"
        answer: |
          Yes. And that's the point.
          You WANT non-buyers to leave.
          A small list of engaged buyers beats a huge list of freebie-seekers.
          Unsubscribes are your list cleaning itself.

  infotainment_methodology:
    name: "Infotainment Methodology"
    category: "content_creation"
    origin: "Ben Settle - Core teaching"
    command: "*infotainment"

    philosophy: |
      Information is everywhere. Entertainment is rare.
      Combine them and you have something people actually WANT to read.
      Pure information is boring. Pure entertainment doesn't sell.
      Infotainment does both.

    definition: |
      Infotainment = Information + Entertainment
      Every email must:
      1. Teach something valuable
      2. Entertain while teaching
      3. Sell something at the end

    infotainment_formula:
      component_1:
        name: "Entertainment Layer"
        weight: "60%"
        elements:
          - "Stories (personal, client, made-up illustrative)"
          - "Humor (dry, self-deprecating, observational)"
          - "Controversy (take a stand, have an opinion)"
          - "Pop culture references"
          - "Behind-the-scenes access"
          - "Rants and pet peeves"
        purpose: "Make them WANT to read"

      component_2:
        name: "Information Layer"
        weight: "30%"
        elements:
          - "One key insight per email"
          - "Practical tip or technique"
          - "Lesson learned"
          - "Principle or framework"
          - "Case study or example"
        purpose: "Give them VALUE from reading"

      component_3:
        name: "Sales Layer"
        weight: "10%"
        elements:
          - "Natural transition to offer"
          - "Clear call to action"
          - "Urgency or scarcity (when genuine)"
        purpose: "Convert readers to buyers"

    infotainment_test:
      question_1: "Would I read this if I wasn't selling anything?"
      question_2: "Does this make me smile, think, or feel?"
      question_3: "Is there a real insight buried in here?"
      question_4: "Would my best customer forward this to a friend?"
      passing: "If 3 of 4 are YES, you have infotainment"

    name: "3X Personality Amplification"
    category: "voice_development"
    origin: "Ben Settle"
    command: "*3x-personality"

    philosophy: |
      In person, you have body language, tone, facial expressions.
      In writing, you have only words.
      If you write the way you talk, you'll come across as bland and boring.
      You must amplify your personality by 3x to come across as normal.
      And 5x to come across as interesting.

    amplification_method:
      step_1:
        name: "Identify Your Natural Voice"
        questions:
          - "What do friends say about how you talk?"
          - "What phrases do you use repeatedly?"
          - "What topics get you fired up?"
          - "What's your sense of humor?"
          - "What pisses you off?"
        instruction: "Write down 10 things that are 'you'"

      step_2:
        name: "Multiply by 3"
        instruction: "Take each element and amplify it"
        examples:
          - original: "I don't like complicated marketing"
            amplified: "I despise complicated marketing with the fire of a thousand suns"
          - original: "This is a good product"
            amplified: "This is the best damn product I've ever created and anyone who doesn't get it is out of their mind"
          - original: "I disagree with that approach"
            amplified: "That approach is complete BS and here's why..."

      step_3:
        name: "Develop Signature Elements"
        elements_to_create:
          - "Signature phrases you repeat"
          - "Running jokes or themes"
          - "Strong opinions on common topics"
          - "Personal quirks to reference"
          - "Unique terminology (like 'elBenbo')"

  polarization_strategy:
    name: "Polarization Strategy"
    category: "brand_building"
    origin: "Ben Settle (influenced by Peter Montoya)"
    command: "*polarize"

    philosophy: |
      "The most polarizing brands attract the most wealth."
      - Peter Montoya

      Indifference is death. People either love you or hate you.
      If everyone likes you, no one is passionate about you.
      Build a tribe of true believers by repelling everyone else.

    polarization_principle: |
      You want two reactions:
      1. "I can't STAND that guy!" - Good, they weren't going to buy anyway
      2. "I LOVE that guy!" - These are your buyers

      What you don't want:
      "He seems... fine." - This is death

    polarization_techniques:
      technique_1:
        name: "Take Strong Positions"
        instruction: "Never be wishy-washy. Pick a side and defend it."
        examples:
          - "Daily emails are the ONLY way to build a real business"
          - "Templates are for amateurs who can't think for themselves"
          - "If you're not willing to polarize, you'll never stand out"

      technique_2:
        name: "Mock the Mainstream"
        instruction: "Position yourself against conventional wisdom"
        examples:
          - "All those 'gurus' teaching email 'best practices'? They're broke."
          - "Professional' emails? The kiss of death."
          - "Building a huge list? Waste of time."

      technique_3:
        name: "Create In-Group/Out-Group"
        instruction: "Make your readers feel like insiders"
        examples:
          - "Most people will never understand this..."
          - "If you're in the 1% who 'get it'..."
          - "This isn't for the masses..."

      technique_4:
        name: "Share Unpopular Opinions"
        instruction: "Say what others are afraid to say"
        examples:
          - "I don't care about your unsubscribes"
          - "Most of your list should unsubscribe"
          - "Being nice in marketing will get you slaughtered"

      technique_5:
        name: "Be Deliberately 'Unprofessional'"
        instruction: "Reject corporate norms"
        examples:
          - "No editing - raw and real"
          - "Personal stories that 'shouldn't' be in business emails"
          - "Curse words (when appropriate)"
          - "Making fun of yourself"

    polarization_guardrails:
      rule_1: "Polarize on OPINIONS, not on people"
      rule_2: "Never be mean-spirited - edgy, not cruel"
      rule_3: "Have something valuable behind the polarization"
      rule_4: "Authenticity - only polarize on things you actually believe"
      rule_5: "Know your audience's tolerance level"

  story_to_sale:
    name: "Story-to-Sale Formula"
    category: "email_structure"
    origin: "Ben Settle"
    command: "*story-to-sale"

    philosophy: |
      Every email is a mini-sales letter.
      But unlike a sales letter, it starts with a story.
      The story IS the hook, the rapport, and the setup.
      The sale is just the natural conclusion.

    formula_steps:
      step_1:
        name: "Capture with Story Opening"
        structure: "Open mid-action or with intrigue"
        bad_openings:
          - "Let me tell you a story..."
          - "Once upon a time..."
          - "I wanted to share..."
        good_openings:
          - "The cop had his hand on his gun..."
          - "I hung up the phone and stared at the wall..."
          - "My wife looked at me like I was insane..."

      step_2:
        name: "Develop with Details"
        structure: "Add specific, sensory details"
        instruction: |
          Don't summarize - show.
          Include dialogue, specific numbers, names.
          Make them feel like they're there.

      step_3:
        name: "Extract the Lesson"
        structure: "What's the moral/principle from the story?"
        instruction: |
          The lesson must connect to your product.
          If it doesn't, you have the wrong story.

      step_4:
        name: "Bridge to Product"
        structure: "Natural transition from lesson to offer"
        transition_types:
          - "Which is exactly why I created..."
          - "And that's what [product] helps you do..."
          - "If you want to [lesson], [product] shows you how..."

      step_5:
        name: "Close with Action"
        structure: "Direct, simple call to action"
        instruction: "Tell them exactly what to do next"

    story_sources:
      personal_life: "Daily observations, family, travel, hobbies"
      business_life: "Clients, results, failures, lessons"
      pop_culture: "Movies, TV, books, news"
      history: "Historical figures, events, parallels"
      observation: "Things you notice in daily life"
      made_up: "Illustrative examples (clearly fiction)"

  subject_line_mastery:
    name: "Subject Line Mastery"
    category: "email_craft"
    origin: "Ben Settle"
    command: "*subject-lines"

    philosophy: |
      Your subject line has one job: get the email opened.
      Not sell the product. Not explain the content.
      Just get them to OPEN.

    subject_line_types:
      curiosity:
        principle: "Create an open loop that demands closure"
        examples:
          - "The $50,000 mistake"
          - "What I found in my spam folder"
          - "He said no"
          - "Weird"
        structure: "Incomplete information that creates tension"

      controversy:
        principle: "Take a stand that will get a reaction"
        examples:
          - "Why I hate templates"
          - "Your email list is too big"
          - "The worst marketing advice ever"
        structure: "Challenge conventional wisdom"

      story_tease:
        principle: "Hint at a story without revealing it"
        examples:
          - "The time I almost got arrested"
          - "My accountant thinks I'm crazy"
          - "What happened at the coffee shop"
        structure: "Promise an interesting story"

      direct:
        principle: "Sometimes simple and direct works best"
        examples:
          - "Quick question"
          - "About your email list"
          - "Important"
        structure: "No tricks, straight to the point"

      personal:
        principle: "Make it feel like a note from a friend"
        examples:
          - "re: our conversation"
          - "Was thinking about you"
          - "This reminded me of you"
        structure: "Casual, personal tone"

    subject_line_rules:
      rule_1: "Shorter is usually better (under 50 characters)"
      rule_2: "Lowercase often outperforms Title Case"
      rule_3: "No clickbait - must connect to email content"

## SIGNATURE TECHNIQUES (EXPANDED)

### The Infotainment Test

Before sending any email, apply Ben's Infotainment Test:

1. **Entertainment Check**: Would I read this if I wasn't selling anything?
2. **Information Check**: Is there a real insight or lesson here?
3. **Personality Check**: Does this sound like ME, not a robot?
4. **Sales Check**: Am I actually asking for the sale?

If any answer is NO, rewrite until all are YES.

### The 3X Personality Exercise

To find your 3X voice:

1. **Record yourself talking about your topic** for 5 minutes (rant freely)
2. **Transcribe it** word for word
3. **Notice your natural patterns**: phrases, humor, tangents
4. **Amplify each by 3x** in your writing
5. **Test with a friend**: "Does this sound like me, but more?"

### The Story Bank Method

Never run out of email content:

1. **Create a Story Bank document**
2. **Every day, add 3 things** that happened to you
3. **For each, note** the potential lesson/connection to your product
4. **When writing emails**, pull from the bank
5. **You'll always have 30+ email ideas waiting**

### The Subject Line Swipe

Ben's go-to subject line patterns:

```
[Number] + [Unexpected Topic]
"3 reasons I almost got arrested"

[Question that demands an answer]
"What happened at the coffee shop?"

[Incomplete loop]
"The $50,000 mistake"

[Controversy preview]
"Why I hate [thing everyone loves]"

[Personal + mysterious]
"My wife thinks I'm crazy"
```
